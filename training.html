Training & Education
Proper awareness is a lot more involved than you may think...

Proper awareness involves compliance, governance, planning, policies, proper development of a plan, and managing your risks.

Programs and Companies that can help train you further:

SysAdmin, Audit, Network, and Security, also known as SANS, provides a variety of information security training options. They even offer options tailored to your preffered format, course, and even location! For more information head to their website: https://www.sans.org/information-security-training

International Information System Security Certification Consortium, also known as (ISC)2 Provides training tailored to your needs. They have classroom based programs, online instructor-led training, and even self paced training. For more information, please follow the link to their website: https://www.isc2.org/

Cybrary is a free open source program that offers a lot more involved training. Example courses include, Introduction to IT & Cybersecurity, Penetration Testing and Ethical Hacking, CCNA certification courses, plus so many more. If you're interested in not only expanding your knowledge but also widening your horizon, visit their site at: https://www.cybrary.it/

EC-Council has over 20 different categories to help you nott only learn the basics of awareness but also provide you with a resource to help push you into the professional field of information security. Some examples of their categories include, certified Network Defender, Forensic Investigator, Disaster Recovery Professional, and Encryption Specialist. If any of those peaked your interest, or you want to investigate futher, visit their site at: https://www.eccouncil.org/

Global Information Assurance Certification or GIAC provides six different categories to help give you the training you need. Some of the topics include, cyber defense, offensive operations, and cloud security. GIAC provides a recognized certification, that can be job specific, so you don't waste time or money, and get a more detailed in-depth look at what really matters to your role. For more information visit their site at: https://www.giac.org/

After all, the best way to avoid risk, is to be aware of it.
