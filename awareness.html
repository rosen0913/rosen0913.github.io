Topics to be aware of:
Phishing

Phishing is a cyber crime where the attacker takes advantage of a user through email, telephone, or text. The attacker poses as a reputable person or company in attempt to gain access to sensative information they recieve from that said individual. The information is then used to gain access to important accounts resulting in a breach of information, identity theft, and potential financial loss.

Thought provoking and too good to be true statements.

Removable media

Removable media poses a risk for theft of information as well ass a threat if the user inserts an unidentified source of media, a program may begin to run and hijack the users device.

Examples of removable media include:

  1. USB sticks
  2. SD cards
  3. CDs
  4. Smartphones
Passwords and Authentication

Avoid the use of simple passwords, recognizable passwords, or simple letter based only passwords. Also, do NOT reuse passwords for multiple accounts! Strong password components include:

  1. 15 or more characters
  2. Mixing of characters! (using numbers, symbols, capitals and lowercase)
  3. Don't use patterned passwords. For example "qwerty" or "2580"
Physical Security

Don't leave running systems unattended, don't leave sticky notes with passwords, keeping important documents under lock and key, and using idle screen locks, with passwords.
