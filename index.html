Life growing up

As a child, I was raised to explore. For the longest time technology was not a part of my family's life. Growing up we had one home phone and old computers with Window's XP that were primarily used by my siblings to play CD ROM games that never involved the internet. We had one home phone, that was corded to the wall, and the only numbers we had were for our close family, like my mother, father, and grandmother. In fact, I didn't get my first computer until my freshman year of high school, and my first basic smartphone came a few years later. So hobbies that I picked up over the years taught me a little about everything. Gardening, photography, woodworking, car repair, you name it. It wasn't until I entered high school that my passion for technology really took off. Starting off I was in a computer hardware repair class, and the feeling of taking everything apart and seeing how all the pieces worked together had me hooked. The passion spiraled into Computer Software, Linux, personal PC builds, Networking, CyberSecurity, and Website Design. There really was no stopping me, and the same could still be said today, I am always looking for new subjects in technology to explore, and learn. All of these things may seem overwhelming, but that is what I love about technology, watching how it continuously evolves over the years and becomes more streamlined, and what once was a twenty-pound laptop that couldn't stray far from an outlet is now a mere pound or two and can go days without a recharge.
